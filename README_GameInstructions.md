Acey Duecy is originally a dice game that originates from the Middle East, Greece or Turkey. 
The exact time frame is unknown, but it is generally believed to be from that area. It gets its name from the best possible hand you can get in the game, an Ace and a Two. 
The Ace is high in this game. 
The strategy is to bet heavily when the gap between the two numbers is the highest.

To play Acey Duecy, the dealer draws three cards, two of which are face up. 
The player then guesses whether the third card is in between the first two cards.
The player wins if he guesses correctly. 
The caveat is that if the third card equals the first or second card, the player loses. 
So, there is never a 100% chance of winning the game, which leads us to the best possible hand. 
As stated above, the best draw possible is an Ace and a Two. 
Unless the third card is an Ace or a Two, the player pretty much has a good shot, and as a result, a lot of players tend to go all in when those two cards show up.
